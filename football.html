Rules of American Football

American football is played between two teams of 11 players each. The objective is to score points by advancing the ball into the opposing team's end zone for a touchdown or kicking the ball through the opponent's goalposts for a field goal.

  • Games are divided into four 15-minute quarters
  • Teams have four downs (attempts) to advance the ball 10 yards
  • A touchdown is worth 6 points, with an extra point attempt afterward
  • A field goal is worth 3 points
  • The team with the most points at the end of the game wins

Traditional Offensive Schemes

Offensive schemes are strategies used to advance the ball and score points. Some common offensive schemes include:

  • Pro-style offense: Balanced attack using both running and passing plays
  • Spread offense: Uses multiple receivers to spread the defense thin
  • West Coast offense: Emphasizes short, high-percentage passes
  • Option offense: Gives the quarterback multiple options to run or pass

Traditional Defensive Schemes

Defensive schemes are strategies used to stop the opposing team from scoring. Common defensive formations include:

  • 4-3 Defense: Four linemen and three linebackers
  • 3-4 Defense: Three linemen and four linebackers
  • Nickel Defense: Replaces a linebacker with an extra defensive back
  • Man-to-Man Coverage: Each defender is assigned to a specific offensive player
  • Zone Coverage: Defenders are responsible for specific areas of the field

Football Positions

Offense:

  • Quarterback (QB): Leader of the offense, throws passes and hands off the ball
  • Running Back (RB): Carries the ball on running plays and blocks for the QB
  • Wide Receiver (WR): Catches passes and blocks for running plays
  • Tight End (TE): Blocks and catches passes, a hybrid position
  • Offensive Line: Protects the QB and creates holes for running plays

Defense:

  • Defensive Line: Rushes the passer and stops the run
  • Linebackers (LB): Versatile players who can rush, tackle, and cover
  • Cornerbacks (CB): Cover wide receivers and defend against passes
  • Safeties: Last line of defense, can play run support or pass coverage
